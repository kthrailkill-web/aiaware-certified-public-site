1. AI Governance & Safety Statement

AI Aware Certified™ teaches AI governance, identity security, and responsible AI practices. We hold ourselves to the same standards we teach. This statement describes how we apply AI governance principles to our own business operations.

1.1 No Training on Customer or Learner Data

We do not train, fine-tune, or evaluate any AI/ML model on:

  • Personal information you provide (name, email, account details)
  • Your course progress, exam responses, or lab submissions
  • Your communications with us (support requests, feedback)
  • Any other learner-specific data we process under our Privacy Policy

1.2 Production Data Stays Out

Our Terms of Use require that you do not upload your employer's production data, real credentials, real PII, or other confidential third-party information to our platform.

Our labs that include "bring your own data" patterns are designed to run on your own work-sanctioned device, in your own work-controlled environment. If a learner uploads production data in violation of our Terms, we may immediately delete it, suspend the account, and notify the affected organization without further notice.

1.3 AI Tools in Course Development

We use AI tools (e.g., large language models) to assist in course content development — drafting outlines, generating practice scenarios, polishing language, and creating supplementary examples. All AI-assisted content is reviewed, edited, and validated by a human author before publication. AI-generated narration uses synthetic voice technology (e.g., Synthesia) with human-authored scripts; we do not use AI to make consequential business decisions about learners (e.g., grading, certification eligibility, refund decisions) — those involve human review.

1.4 Vendor and Sub-Processor Choices

We choose vendors who maintain their own security and compliance posture. Our primary processors and the data they handle are listed in our Privacy Policy. We review vendor security and data handling practices before engagement and will update this statement and our Privacy Policy when material changes occur.

1.5 No Sale of Personal Information

We do not sell or rent personal information. We do not engage in cross-context behavioral advertising. Marketing emails are sent only with your explicit opt-in consent and include a clear unsubscribe link in every message.

1.6 Brand Voice and Compliance Framing

Our courses are intentionally non-prescriptive. We do not say "do this and you are compliant." We say "here is the framework, here is how it has played out in practice, here are the questions to ask in your environment." Compliance attestation is the role of your auditor, not your instructor. See our full Disclaimer.

1.7 Updates and Contact

We will update this statement as our practices evolve. Material changes will be posted with an updated effective date. Questions about our AI governance practices: aiaware@aiawarecertified.com.

2. Certificate Issuance Policy

This policy describes how AI Aware LLC issues, verifies, and, when necessary, revokes certificates earned through our training programs. Certificates are issued through the LearnWorlds learning platform.

2.1 Requirements for Certificate Issuance

A certificate of completion is issued when a learner has:

  • Completed all required modules of the course (per LearnWorlds progress tracking)
  • Passed the capstone exam at the required passing score
  • Met any identity verification requirements disclosed at the time of purchase, which may include email confirmation, platform login validation, or additional verification steps disclosed at purchase
  • Maintained an account in good standing (no fraud, cheating, or material breach of our Terms of Use)

2.2 No Automated Decision-Making

Certificates are never issued, denied, or revoked by automated systems alone. All consequential decisions about issuance, denial, revocation, or appeal involve human review. Automated systems (e.g., LearnWorlds progress tracking and exam grading) inform but do not finalize these decisions.

2.3 Name on Certificate, Transferability, and Re-Issuance

Name on certificate: certificates are issued under the name associated with the learner's account at the time of issuance. Requests for name changes may require identity verification.

Transferability: certificates are issued to an individual learner and are not transferable to any other person.

Re-issuance: learners may request a re-issued certificate (e.g., after a name change or loss of original) by contacting aiaware@aiawarecertified.com. We may require identity verification before re-issuing a certificate.

2.4 Verification & Platform Dependency

Each certificate has a unique verification mechanism provided by LearnWorlds. Employers, recruiters, and verifiers can confirm authenticity through that verification path. Certificate metadata includes the learner's name, the course completed, the issuance date, and (where applicable) a unique credential identifier.

Platform dependency: certificate verification is provided through the LearnWorlds platform. Availability or functionality of verification tools may depend on LearnWorlds' systems and may change if we migrate to a different platform in the future.

2.5 Validity and Expiration

AI Aware Certified™ certificates do not expire. Once issued, the certificate remains permanently verifiable. We may add continuing-education or recertification programs in the future; if so, those will be optional unless otherwise disclosed at purchase.

2.6 Revocation and Appeal

We reserve the right to revoke a certificate in cases of:

  • Demonstrated fraud or misrepresentation in the certification process (e.g., another person took the exam, falsified identity)
  • Violation of our Terms of Use related to acceptable use, account sharing, or content misuse
  • Material breach of integrity that we determine, in our reasonable discretion, undermines the credential

Revocation is a serious step and is taken sparingly. Affected learners will be notified via the email on file and given an opportunity to respond before final revocation, except in cases of clear fraud where immediate revocation is warranted. Revoked certificates are removed from the verification system.

Appeal: learners may appeal a revocation decision by submitting additional information to aiaware@aiawarecertified.com within 14 days of the revocation notice. Appeals are reviewed by a human; we will respond with a final decision within 30 days of receiving the appeal.

2.7 Disclaimer on Compliance Outcomes

A certificate of completion does not certify that you, your organization, or your products are compliant with any law, regulation, or framework. See our full Disclaimer.

2.8 Updates and Contact

This policy may be updated as our certification programs evolve. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. For verification of a certificate, appeals, or questions about this policy: aiaware@aiawarecertified.com.

3. Cookie Notice

This Cookie Notice applies to the AI Aware Certified™ marketing website (aiawarecertified.com) and learning platform (academy.aiawarecertified.com). It explains what cookies and similar technologies we use, why we use them, the lawful basis for using them, and your choices for managing them. It supplements our Privacy Policy.

3.1 What Cookies and Similar Technologies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting; third-party cookies are set by a different domain (e.g., a service provider we use). We treat the following similar technologies the same way under this Notice:

  • Local storage and session storage (in-browser data stores)
  • Web beacons / pixels (small images used to detect activity)
  • Software development kits (SDKs) used by our platform providers

3.2 Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep you logged in and remember your course progress
  • Secure the platform against fraud and abuse
  • Process payments through our payment provider
  • Understand site and platform performance so we can improve the learner experience
  • Remember your cookie consent preferences so we don't re-prompt unnecessarily

3.3 Categories and Lawful Basis

We classify cookies into two categories with distinct lawful bases:

  • Strictly Necessary — required for core functionality (login, security, payment session). Cannot be disabled and do not require consent under GDPR / ePrivacy.
  • Analytics / Performance — help us understand how the site and platform are used. Used only with your consent in the EU, UK, and other jurisdictions where consent is required. In the US, these are enabled by default with the option to opt out.

What we do NOT use:

  • Third-party advertising cookies
  • Cross-context behavioral advertising trackers
  • Social media tracking pixels (Facebook Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag, X Pixel, etc.) on our marketing site
  • Profiling cookies for automated decision-making

Some cookies are set directly by LearnWorlds as part of the platform's core functionality (login, video playback, course progress, payment session). For full disclosure of cookies set inside the LearnWorlds academy platform, see LearnWorlds' own Cookie Policy.

3.6 Cookie Banner & How We Get Your Consent

EU, UK, and similar jurisdictions: a cookie banner appears on your first visit. You can:

  • Accept all — strictly necessary + analytics cookies are enabled
  • Reject non-essential — only strictly necessary cookies are enabled
  • Manage preferences — granular per-category control

Your choice is recorded in a first-party consent cookie and respected on future visits. We re-prompt for consent at least every 12 months, or sooner if our cookie usage materially changes.

United States and other jurisdictions: analytics cookies are enabled by default. You can opt out at any time via the cookie settings link in our footer or by enabling Global Privacy Control in your browser.

3.7 Your Choices & Controls

Cookie settings link: available in the site footer at any time — lets you change or withdraw your consent.

Browser controls: you can configure your browser to block or delete cookies. Disabling strictly necessary cookies will break core functionality (you won't stay logged in, payment may fail, video may not play).

Global Privacy Control (GPC): we honor GPC signals where they are reliably detected by our platforms, treating GPC as an opt-out of analytics cookies for that visitor.

Do Not Sell or Share: we do not sell personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising. There is therefore no "Do Not Sell" toggle to engage — but you retain the right to make this request, and it will be honored automatically by our existing practices.

3.8 Retention & Consequences of Rejection

Retention: cookies remain on your device for different periods depending on whether they are session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent cookies (which remain until they expire or are deleted). Specific durations are listed in the table in Section 3.4.

If you reject non-essential cookies:

  • Strictly necessary cookies still function (you can still log in, take courses, receive certificates)
  • We will not collect analytics data from your visits
  • Some performance optimizations may be unavailable

If you reject strictly necessary cookies via your browser: the platform will not function correctly — you will not be able to log in, save progress, or complete a purchase.

3.9 Updates and Contact

We will update this Cookie Notice as our cookie usage changes (e.g., if we add a new analytics tool or remove a vendor). The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Questions about cookies, or to make a privacy/consent request: aiaware@aiawarecertified.com.
